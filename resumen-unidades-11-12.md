## UNIDAD 11 — El pasado: personas, lugares y cosas

### 11.1 · Gramática: Statements with *was* and *were*

Usamos **was / were** para hablar de personas, lugares y cosas en el **pasado**.

| Sujeto | Afirmativa | Negativa |
|--------|-----------|---------|
| I | I **was** in the yard. | I **wasn't** ready. |
| He / She / It | She **was** tired. | It **wasn't** old. |
| You / We / They | We **were** on vacation. | My parents **weren't** there. |

> ⚠️ **was** = singular (I, he, she, it)
> ⚠️ **were** = plural (you, we, they)

**Ejemplos del libro:**
- *I was eight. This was in the summer.*
- *We were on vacation at my grandparents' house.*
- *My parents weren't there.*
- *It wasn't old.*

---

### 11.2 · Gramática: Questions with *was* and *were*

| Tipo | Estructura | Ejemplo |
|------|-----------|---------|
| Yes/No | **Was/Were** + sujeto + …? | *Were you at home?* → No, I wasn't. |
| Yes/No | | *Was the phone in the kitchen?* → Yes, it was. |
| Información | **Wh-** + **was/were** + sujeto | *How old **were** you?* → I was two. |
| Información | | *What color **were** the walls?* → They were orange. |

**Práctica del libro (reordena las palabras):**
1. color / your old phone? / was / What → *What color was your old phone?*
2. was / the phone? / Where → *Where was the phone?*
3. in the kitchen? / the refrigerator / Was → *Was the refrigerator in the kitchen?*
4. the walls / color / in the kitchen? / were / What → *What color were the walls in the kitchen?*
5. big? / Were / the bedrooms → *Were the bedrooms big?*
6. your home / nice? / Was → *Was your home nice?*

## UNIDAD 12 — Comida y restaurantes

### 12.1 · Gramática: Simple past statements (afirmativo y negativo)

#### ✅ Afirmativo — verbos irregulares
El pasado de los verbos irregulares **no termina en -ed**:

| Infinitivo | Pasado simple | Ejemplo |
|-----------|--------------|---------|
| eat | **ate** | I **ate** a sandwich. |
| drink | **drank** | I **drank** water. |
| have | **had** | He **had** soup. |
| go | **went** | I **went** to a food stand. |
| take | **took** | I **took** the bus. |
| buy | **bought** | I **bought** a sandwich. |
| want | **wanted** | She **wanted** an orange. *(regular)* |

#### ❌ Negativo — didn't + verbo base
Después de **didn't**, el verbo principal **siempre va en forma base** (infinitivo sin *to*):

| ✅ Correcto | ❌ Error común |
|-----------|--------------|
| I **didn't eat** a lot. | ~~I didn't ate~~ |
| She **didn't like** the sandwich. | ~~She didn't liked~~ |
| They **didn't drink** the coffee. | ~~They didn't drank~~ |

> 💡 **Regla clave:** `didn't` ya marca el pasado — el verbo siguiente NO cambia.

**Ejemplos del libro:**
- *Yesterday, I took the bus from Aracaju.*
- *I didn't eat a lot for breakfast.*
- *She didn't like the sandwich.*
- *They didn't drink the coffee.*

---

### 12.2 · Gramática: Simple past questions + *any*

#### Preguntas Yes/No → **Did** + sujeto + verbo base
| Ejemplo | Respuesta |
|---------|----------|
| *Did you have apple juice?* | Yes, I did. / No, I didn't. |
| *Did she like the fish?* | Yes, she did. / No, she didn't. |
| *Did they eat any ice cream?* | Yes, they did. / No, they didn't. |

#### Preguntas de información → **Wh- + did** + sujeto + verbo base
| Ejemplo |
|---------|
| *How did you hear about us?* |
| *What did they eat?* |
| *Where did you go last night?* |

#### *Some* vs *Any*

| | Cuándo usarlo | Ejemplo |
|---|---|---|
| **some** | oraciones afirmativas | *I had **some** soup.* |
| **some** | cuando es un pedido/oferta | *Can I have **some** bread?* ✅ |
| **any** | preguntas yes/no | *Did you have **any** dessert?* |
| **any** | oraciones negativas | *They didn't have **any** juice.* |

> ⚠️ `Can I have any bread?` ❌ → `Can I have some bread?` ✅ *(en pedidos, usa some)*

### Página 7 · 12.1 — Gramática: Simple past statements (reglas completas)

#### Las 4 reglas del libro

| # | Regla |
|---|-------|
| 1 | Usa el simple past para hablar de eventos **terminados** (pasado). |
| 2 | Los verbos simples en pasado pueden tener la **misma ortografía** (irregulares: *cut, put, read*) o **diferente** (*go → went*). |
| 3 | La mayoría de verbos **regulares**: añadir **-d** o **-ed** (*want → wanted, arrive → arrived*). |
| 4 | Para hacer la **negación**: `didn't` + verbo base (*didn't eat, didn't drink*). |

#### Verbos irregulares del libro — lista clave

| Infinitivo | Pasado simple | ❌ Forma incorrecta |
|-----------|--------------|---------------------|
| eat | **ate** | ~~eated~~ |
| drink | **drank** | ~~drinked~~ |
| have | **had** | ~~haved~~ |
| go | **went** | ~~goed~~ |
| take | **took** | ~~taked~~ |
| buy | **bought** | ~~buyed~~ |

#### Ejercicio del libro — respuestas correctas

| Oración | Correcto |
|---------|---------|
| It was a big sandwich, but I _ate_ / eat it all. | **ate** |
| We _have_ / **had** fish for dinner last night. | **had** |
| She didn't _buy_ / **bought** food at the supermarket. | **buy** (después de *didn't*, siempre base) |
| I was hungry and really _needed_ / need some food. | **needed** (evento terminado) |
| We _arrive_ / **arrived** at the restaurant at 5:30. | **arrived** (regular) |
| I didn't _tried_ / **try** it. | **try** (base después de *didn't*) |

> 💡 **Regla de oro:** Después de `didn't`, el verbo SIEMPRE va en **forma base**.
> ✅ *She didn't **buy** food.* ← NO *didn't bought*

#### Frases para hablar de tu propio pasado (ejercicio del libro)

- *For breakfast, I ate ___ and I drank ___.*
- *Last week, I bought ___ at the supermarket.*
- *The last movie I watched was ___.*
- *Last weekend, I went to ___ with ___.*

### Página 9 · 12.4 — Writing: Reseña de restaurante

#### Modelo del libro — *Fish Around* (Los Ángeles)

**Reseña de Frank B.** ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
> *This is a nice restaurant. It's big and it has a lot of windows, so it's very light. I had vegetable soup, fish, and rice. The soup was good. I love fish, and the fish was great! My brother had fish and vegetables, and he liked his meal, too. We both had dessert. He had cake and I had ice cream. The servers were so friendly. We were really happy with our meal, and it wasn't expensive.*

**Reseña de Julieta F.** ⭐⭐⭐⭐
> *I think this is a good restaurant, but I didn't choose it. My friends like fish, so they wanted to eat here. I like meat, but there wasn't any meat on the menu. The food? Well, my friends liked it. They had fish, vegetables, rice, and dessert. I had fish and potatoes. The potatoes were OK. For dessert, I had pineapple cake and coconut ice cream. Wow! It was so good! The servers were nice. The price was OK — not cheap, but not expensive.*

#### Qué incluir en una reseña (Writing checklist)

| Elemento | Preguntas que puedes responder |
|----------|-------------------------------|
| **Apariencia** | Is it big/small/light/dark? |
| **Comida** | What did you eat? Was it good? |
| **Tu opinión** | Did you like the food? Why? |
| **El servicio** | Were the servers friendly/nice? |
| **El precio** | Was it expensive? Was the price OK? |

#### Register Check — escritura informal

En textos informales puedes **hacerte preguntas y responderlas** tú mismo:
> *Was the food good? Well, my friends liked it.*
> *The fish was… well, it was fish!*

### Página 10 · Writing Skills — Cómo escribir listas en inglés

#### Reglas del libro

| Cantidad de elementos | Cómo unirlos | Ejemplo |
|----------------------|-------------|---------|
| **2 elementos** | Usar **and** (sin coma) | *My brother had fish **and** vegetables.* |
| **3 o más elementos** | Usar **coma** entre cada uno + **and** antes del último | *I had vegetable soup**,** fish**,** and rice.* |

#### ✅ Ejemplos correctos del libro

- *I had fish **and** rice.* ← 2 cosas, solo *and*
- *I had vegetable soup**,** fish**,** and rice.* ← 3 cosas, coma + *and*
- *They had fish**,** vegetables**,** rice**,** and dessert.* ← 4 cosas

#### ❌ Errores comunes

| Error | Corrección |
|-------|-----------|
| *I had fish, and rice.* | *I had fish **and** rice.* (2 elementos: no lleva coma) |
| *I had soup fish and rice.* | *I had soup**,** fish**,** and rice.* (3+: necesita comas) |

#### Frases modelo para escribir TU reseña

**Apertura:**
- *This is a nice/great/small restaurant.*
- *It's in [lugar]. It's [descripción].*

**La comida:**
- *I had [comida 1] and [comida 2].*
- *I had [comida 1], [comida 2], and [comida 3].*
- *The [comida] was great/good/OK/awful.*
- *I didn't like the [comida].*

**El servicio y precio:**
- *The servers were friendly/nice/helpful.*
- *It wasn't expensive. / The price was OK.*
- *I was really happy with my meal.*

**Cierre:**
- *I'm giving this restaurant [número] stars because [razón].*
- *Would you like to eat here? I think you should try it!*

### Página 12 · 11.3 — Pronunciación: /oʊ/ vs /ɑː/

Dos sonidos vocálicos muy diferentes en inglés:

| Sonido | Ejemplo | Palabras del libro |
|--------|---------|-------------------|
| **/oʊ/** | **know** | don't, go, home, know |
| **/ɑː/** | **not** | on, no, concert |

> 💡 **/oʊ/** suena como "ou" en español (como en "soy")
> 💡 **/ɑː/** suena como una "a" abierta y corta (como la "o" en "hot" en inglés americano)

#### Estrategia real: Taking time to think (ganar tiempo para pensar)

Cuando necesitas un momento para pensar antes de responder, usa estas expresiones naturales:

| Expresión | Uso |
|-----------|-----|
| *Let me think…* | Déjame pensar… |
| *Um…* | Mmm… (pausa natural) |
| *Uh…* | Eh… (pausa natural) |
| *I have no idea.* | No tengo ni idea. |
| *Maybe it's…?* | ¿Quizás es…? |

**Ejemplo del libro:**
> *Um. I have no idea. Let me think. Maybe it's Kate Hudson?*

#### Conversaciones de práctica del libro

| Conversación | Frases clave |
|-------------|-------------|
| *Was Mona at the concert yesterday?* → *No, she **wasn't**. She **was** at home.* | was / wasn't |
| *Where is Leonardo DiCaprio from?* → *I have no idea.* | expresar incertidumbre |
| *Don't go! Sorry, I have to go home.* | have to = tener que |
| *Are we on the right bus? It's very slow.* → *I don't know.* | incertidumbre |

> There was also a lot of homework from my school days in the box. Hey, I was smart! Well, usually. My math homework was awful! 😱 I don't think I want to keep it.
>
> Your old soccer ball was in the box, too. ⚽ Why do I have it? I don't know. There's some writing on it. Maybe it's the autograph of a famous soccer player. Do you want it?
>
> Love, Angie

#### Writing Skills — La oración temática (Topic sentence)

> 📌 La **topic sentence** le dice al lector **de qué trata el párrafo**.

| Párrafo | Topic sentence |
|---------|---------------|
| Párrafo 1 (postal) | *Do you remember this postcard?* |
| Párrafo 2 (tarea) | *There was also a lot of homework from my school days in the box.* |
| Párrafo 3 (pelota) | *Your old soccer ball was in the box, too.* |

> 💡 Cada párrafo trata **un solo tema**. Por eso el email tiene tres párrafos — porque habla de tres cosas distintas.

#### Register Check — Uso de emojis

| ✅ Puedes usar emojis en… | ❌ NO uses emojis en… |
|--------------------------|----------------------|
| Textos (mensajes de texto) | Emails formales (trabajo) |
| Redes sociales | Emails para la universidad/college |
| Emails informales (amigos, familia) | Documentos oficiales |

**Ejemplo del libro:**
> *My math homework was awful!* 😱 ← informal, OK
> *My math homework was awful.* ← formal, sin emoji

#### Estructura para escribir TU propio email

**Tarea del libro:** *Imagine you have a box of old things from your childhood. Write an email to a friend about two or three things in the box. Write a topic sentence for each paragraph.*

```
Párrafo 1: [objeto 1]
  Topic sentence: Do you remember this ___?
  Detalle: It was ___ . It was from ___ in ___ .

Párrafo 2: [objeto 2]
  Topic sentence: There was also a ___ in the box.
```

### Página 17 · 11.1 — Gramática: Statements with *was* and *were*

#### Cuadro gramatical del libro

| Afirmativo | Negativo |
|-----------|---------|
| I was in the yard. | My parents **weren't** there. |
| We were on vacation. | It **wasn't** old. |

#### Reglas clave (ejercicio del libro)

| # | Regla |
|---|-------|
| 1 | Usamos *was* y *were* para hablar de personas, lugares o cosas en el **pasado**. |
| 2 | *Was* y *were* son las formas pasadas de **be** (no de *go*). |
| 3 | *Was* y *were* son formas **afirmativas**. |
| 4 | *Wasn't* y *weren't* son formas **negativas**. |

#### Texto de práctica completo — Ethan Collins (Facebook)

Texto original con respuestas correctas (rellenar con *was, wasn't, were, weren't*):

> I remember a visit to my uncle's farm in August 1998. It **¹ was** a big farm — just a nice, small one. There **² weren't** a lot of animals, only ten or fifteen. They **³ were** farm animals, not pets, so they **⁴ weren't** friendly, but my uncle's dog **⁵ was** really nice. He **⁶ was** only two or three, so he **⁷ wasn't** very old, but he **⁸ was** smart. His real name **⁹ was** Jake, but my name for him **¹⁰ was** "Fluffy Duffy." It's an awful name, but I **¹¹ was** seven at the time, and to me, Fluffy Duffy **¹² was** a beautiful name! We **¹³ were** great friends.
> #flashbackfriday #1998 #bestfriend

### Página 20 · 11.2 — Gramática: Questions with *was* and *were*

#### Cuadro gramatical del libro

| Tipo | Estructura | Ejemplo | Respuesta |
|------|-----------|---------|----------|
| **Yes/No** | Was/Were + sujeto + …? | *Were you at home?* | No, I wasn't. |
| **Yes/No** | | *Was the phone in the kitchen?* | Yes, it was. |
| **Información** | Wh- + was/were + sujeto | *How old **were** you?* | I was two. |
| **Información** | | *What color **were** the walls?* | They were orange. |

> ⚠️ En preguntas yes/no: *was/were* va al **principio**.
> ⚠️ En preguntas de información: *was/were* va **después** de la palabra interrogativa.

#### Reglas del libro (ejercicio de opción múltiple)

| # | Pregunta | Respuesta |
|---|----------|-----------|
| 1 | In yes/no questions, *was* and *were* go at the… | **beginning** of the question |
| 2 | In information questions, *was* and *were* go… | **before** the subject (after the question word) |

#### Ejercicio: ordenar palabras para hacer preguntas

| Palabras | Pregunta correcta |
|----------|------------------|
| color / your old phone? / was / What | *What color was your old phone?* |
| was / the phone? / Where | *Where was the phone?* |
| in the kitchen? / the refrigerator / Was | *Was the refrigerator in the kitchen?* |
| the walls / color / in the kitchen? / were / What | *What color were the walls in the kitchen?* |
| big? / Were / the bedrooms | *Were the bedrooms big?* |
| your home / nice? / Was | *Was your home nice?* |
